The XOR sum of a list is the bitwise XOR of all its elements. If the
list only contains one element, then its XOR sum will be equal to this
element.

#+begin_html
  </p>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <ul>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <li>
#+end_html

For example, the XOR sum of [1,2,3,4] is equal to 1 XOR 2 XOR 3 XOR 4 =
4, and the XOR sum of [3] is equal to 3.

#+begin_html
  </li>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  </ul>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <p>
#+end_html

You are given two 0-indexed arrays arr1 and arr2 that consist only of
non-negative integers.

#+begin_html
  </p>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <p>
#+end_html

Consider the list containing the result of arr1[i] AND arr2[j] (bitwise
AND) for every (i, j) pair where 0 <= i < arr1.length and 0 <= j <
arr2.length.

#+begin_html
  </p>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <p>
#+end_html

Return the XOR sum of the aforementioned list.

#+begin_html
  </p>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <p>
#+end_html

 

#+begin_html
  </p>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <p>
#+end_html

Example 1:

#+begin_html
  </p>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <pre>
  <strong>Input:</strong> arr1 = [1,2,3], arr2 = [6,5]
  <strong>Output:</strong> 0
  <strong>Explanation:</strong> The list = [1 AND 6, 1 AND 5, 2 AND 6, 2 AND 5, 3 AND 6, 3 AND 5] = [0,1,2,0,2,1].
  The XOR sum = 0 XOR 1 XOR 2 XOR 0 XOR 2 XOR 1 = 0.
  </pre>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  <p>
#+end_html

#+begin_html
  </p>
#+end_html

#+begin_html

  <strong>Input:</strong> arr1 = [12], arr2 = [4]
  <strong>Output:</strong> 4
  <strong>Explanation:</strong> The list = [12 AND 4] = [4]. The XOR sum = 4.
  </pre>
#+end_html
